Cultivation Tips For Wheat
Details
Farming Methods
Land
Medium to heavy, well-drained soils favor wheat crops. Heavy and deep soil should be selected for irrigated wheat. Good yield of wheat can be obtained if chemical fertilizers are mixed with rich soil in medium type of soil. While sowing wheat, it should be planted in heavy soil so that rain water moisture is retained for maximum time and helps in getting more yield.
Pre-cultivation
15-20 cm of soil after kharif harvesting. Plow deep. After that 3-4 layers of kulwa should be given. Before the last threshing, 10-12 bullock cartloads of well-rotted cow dung or compost should be spread over the field.
Improved varieties
Trimbak, Tapovan, Sarbati variety MCS 6122 and Bakshi variety Godavari should be selected for sowing at the time of horticulture. NIAW 34, AKW 4627 varieties are useful for horticultural late sowing and Panchvati, Sharad varieties for arable sowing. If the availability of water is less, Netravati variety should be planted.
seed
8 to 8.8 lakh plants per acre should be in the field. To maintain this quantity of plants, horticulture requires 40-50 kg of seed for early sowing and 50-60 kg of seed for late sowing.
seeding process
Seed treatment should be done at the rate of captan or thiram 3 g per kg. Also, 250 grams of Azotobacter and 250 grams of PSB should be treated with bacterial fertilizer for every 10 kg of seeds.
sowing
Sowing should preferably be done in south-east direction. Timely sowing of horticultural crop in two rows 20 cm. and late sowing 18 cm. Keep a distance. Wheat sowing is shallow i.e. 5-6 cm. To be done on the room. So the germination is good. Arable wheat is sown in two rows 20 cm apart. Keep a distance. To cover the seed, the harrow should be run upside down so that the seed is well buried in the soil and covered with soil. Keeping in mind the slope of the land, the size of 2.5 to 4 meters wide and 7 to 25 meters long should be laid for wheat.
Manure management
48 kg of nitrogen, 24 kg of phosphorus and 16 kg of palash should be given per acre for timely sowing of irrigated wheat. At the time of sowing, half the amount of nitrogen and full amount of phosphorus and palash should be given. The remaining half of the nitrogen should be applied three weeks after sowing, after weeding at the crown budding stage. 32 kg of nitrogen, 16 kg of phosphorus and 16 kg of palash should be applied per acre for late sowing of irrigated wheat.
At the time of sowing, half the amount of nitrogen and full amount of phosphorus and palash should be given. The remaining half of the nitrogen should be applied three weeks after sowing. For arable wheat, 16 kg of nitrogen and 8 kg of phosphorus should be sown per acre. Apart from this, spraying of 2 percent urea solution should be done at grain filling stage i.e. after 65-70 days.
Intercultivation
The land should be freed by weeding once or twice, as well as hoeing.
For weed control, Isoproteuron 0.8-1.2 kg or metsulfuron methyl 8 g or 2,4-D 250-500 ml per acre should be sprayed 30-35 days after sowing.
Water management
Generally watering should be done at an interval of 18-21 days, medium to heavy soils require 4 to 5 waterings for crop establishment.
Susceptible stage of wheat crop to water cycle (days after sowing): Eruption stage due to crown - 18 to 21, Stamen bearing stage - 40 to 45, Flowering and chive filling stage - 60 to 65, Grain filling stage - 80 to 85
Crop protection
Tambera : Wheat crop should not be sown before or late in the season. The quantity of chemical fertilizers should be balanced. Avoid overwatering the crop. Propiconazole 1 ml as soon as the disease appears. Or Mancozeb mixed with two grams per liter of water should be sprayed.
Soot or grit: After seed treatment Carbendazim 1 or less at the rate of Thiram 3 per kg of seed. Diseases in standing crops should be eradicated below the umbilicus. Diseases caused by ear disease destroy trees.
Mawa and Tudtude: Thiamithoxam 1 or Acetamiprid 5 per 10 results two sprays at 10-15 days interval. Biological remedies include Verticillium Lecani or Metarhizium 50 each for 10 days at 10-15 degrees
Harvesting and production
Early planting of horticultural wheat yields 18-20 quintals per acre, late planting of horticultural wheat yields 14-16 quintals per acre and field planting yields 4.8-6 quintals per acre.
