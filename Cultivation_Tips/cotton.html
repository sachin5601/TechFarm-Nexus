Cultivation Tips For Cotton
Details
Farming Methods
Land
Medium to heavy for planting, 60 to 120 cm. Choose deep and well-drained, well-drained soil with a pH of 5.5-8.5.
Pre-cultivation
Plowing should be done before planting. After the soil is slightly heated and dried, 2-3 rows of kulwa should be cut. 4 tonnes per acre for irrigated cotton and 2 tonnes per acre for dryland cotton should be spread over the field and mixed with the soil before the last transplant. If available, vermicompost should be mixed with 1 ton of cow dung/compost per acre.
Varieties
Horticulture : LRA 5166, JLH 168, Phule Yamuna, Phule Rakhumai, , H10, Phule Tarang, DCH 32, Phule 388, Phule Dhara, NHH-454, PH-316, 348 Hybrid (Non Bt)
Drywater : Indigenous improved varieties Y-1, Phule 794, JLA 505, AKA 5, 7, 8 and 8401, Turab, Phule Anmol, Phule Dhanvantari
B. T. : B. T. While selecting the cotton variety, one should choose the variety which is resistant to the sap-sucking insect and red rot, which is resistant to the local conditions, consistent in production and demanded by the traders.
Cultivation
Seed : 1-1.2 kg/acre
Cultivation of horticultural cotton should be done from the second fortnight of May to the first week of June. Dryland cotton should be sown after 3-4 inches of monsoon rainfall.
Seed processing
Before planting, treat with 25 grams each of Phosphorus Solubilizing Bacteria (PSB), Azotobacter along with Trichoderma at the rate of 4 grams per kg of seed
Planting distance
Bt Cotton Horticulture: West Maharashtra, Khandesh 120 x 60 cm, Marathwada 150 x 30, 180 x 30 cm
Bt cotton dryland: West Maharashtra, Khandesh 90 x 90, 90 x 60 cm, Marathwada 120 x 45 cm, Vidarbha 90 x 45 cm
weed control
After 3 and 6 weeks after planting, weeding should be done and 3-4 weeding should be done.
For cotton, after planting but before emergence, 30-40 ml pendimethylene mixed with 10 liters of water should be evenly sprayed on the soil. Or spray pyrithiobac sodium 12.5 ml plus/or quizalofop ethyl 10 ml per 10 liters of water 21-28 days after planting when the stems are at 2-4 leaves. If herbicides are used, a weeding and weeding should be done 6 weeks after planting.
Water management
Cotton requires 20% of its total water requirement from germination to tillering, 40% from tillering to flowering, 30% from flowering to tillering and 10% till final picking. Water stress should not be allowed during the sensitive stages of germination, budding, flowering, holding and filling of pots. Water should be stopped after 30-40 percent of the buds on the tree have burst
Crop protection
Kavadi: Spray with copper oxychloride or zainab 2.5 gm/ liter of water.
Death: Before planting, the seed should be treated with Trichoderma 4 gm or Thirum 3 gm/kg seed. Apply Trichoderma 4 gm or copper oxychloride 2.5 gm/ liter of water as soon as infestation appears.
Mava, Tudtude, Aphids: Monocrotophos 25 ml or Acephate 20 gm or Acetamiprid 4 gm or Thimethoxam 2.5 gm or Flonicamid 3 gm per 10 liters of water should be sprayed.
White fly: If the above insecticides do not control, spray with nimboli oil 50 ml or trizophos 2 ml or diphenthiuron 12 gm with 15 gm nirma powder.
Collection and storage
Harvest at approximately 30-35% bud break. After that 2-3 readings should be done at an interval of 15-20 days. Cotton of different varieties and varieties should be picked separately and stored separately. After picking, the cotton should be exposed to the sun for 3-4 days and should be stored cleanly and divided according to grading.
